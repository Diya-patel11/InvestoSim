The Power of Compound Interest

Compound interest is often called the "eighth wonder of the world" (attributed to Einstein) because it allows your money to grow exponentially over time. Understanding this concept is crucial for long-term wealth building.

What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is the interest you earn on both your original investment and on the interest that accumulates. Unlike simple interest (which only grows linearly), compound interest grows exponentially because you earn "interest on interest."

The Rule of 72

A quick way to estimate how long it takes your money to double:

72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double

  • At 6% return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
  • At 8% return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double
  • At 12% return: 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double

How to Maximize Compound Growth

  • Start as early as possible - Time is your greatest ally
  • Be consistent - Regular investments (SIPs) work best
  • Reinvest dividends - Let all earnings compound
  • Avoid withdrawing - Breaking the cycle reduces final value dramatically
  • Increase amounts over time - As your income grows, increase investments

The best time to start investing was yesterday. The second best time is today. Even small amounts invested early can grow to surprising sums.

Key Takeaways

  • Compound growth accelerates over time - the longer you stay invested, the faster your money grows
