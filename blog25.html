Building Your First Portfolio

A well-constructed portfolio is your financial foundation. Learn how to create a diversified investment portfolio tailored to your goals and risk tolerance.

Step 1: Define Your Goals

Goal Identification

  • Retirement planning (long-term)
  • House purchase (medium-term)
  • Child's education (long-term)
  • Emergency fund (short-term)

Time Horizon

Allocate assets based on when you'll need the money:

Timeframe Suggested Equity Allocation
Short-term (<3 years) 0-20%
Medium-term (3-7 years) 30-60%
Long-term (>7 years) 70-100%

Step 2: Asset Allocation

Core Components

  • Equities: For growth (stocks, equity funds)
  • Fixed Income: For stability (bonds, debt funds)
  • Cash: For liquidity and emergencies
  • Alternatives: For diversification (gold, REITs)

Sample Beginner Portfolios

Risk Profile Equity Debt Gold
Conservative 40% 50% 10%
Moderate 60% 35% 5%
Aggressive 80% 15% 5%

Step 3: Rebalancing Strategy

Rebalancing maintains your target allocation:

  • Review portfolio every 6-12 months
  • Sell assets that have appreciated beyond target
  • Buy assets that are below target allocation
  • Helps "buy low, sell high" systematically
"Diversification is protection against ignorance. It makes little sense if you know what you are doing." — Warren Buffett
