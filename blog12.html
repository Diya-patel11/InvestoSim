Fundamental vs. Technical Analysis

Two primary schools of thought guide investment decisions. Understanding both can make you a more versatile investor and help you choose the approach that fits your style.

Fundamental Analysis

Evaluates a company's intrinsic value by examining:

  • Financial Statements: Balance sheet, income statement, cash flow
  • Management Quality: Track record, corporate governance
  • Industry Position: Market share, competitive advantages
  • Economic Factors: Sector growth, macroeconomic conditions

Indian Context: Look for companies with strong ROE (Return on Equity), low debt, and consistent earnings growth. Check SEBI filings on their website for reliable data.

Technical Analysis

Studies price movements and market psychology through:

  • Price Charts: Candlestick patterns, trends, support/resistance
  • Indicators: Moving averages, RSI, MACD
  • Volume Analysis: Buying/selling pressure
  • Market Sentiment: Fear/greed indicators
"In the short run, the market is a voting machine but in the long run, it is a weighing machine." — Benjamin Graham

Key Differences

Aspect Fundamental Analysis Technical Analysis
Time Horizon Long-term (years) Short-medium term (days-months)
Data Used Financial reports, economic data Price charts, volume data
Best For Value investing, portfolio building Timing entries/exits, trading
Indian Examples Warren Buffett style investing in HDFC, TCS Day trading Nifty futures, swing trading stocks
Tools Screener.in, Annual reports TradingView, Chartink

When to Use Each Approach

  • Use Fundamental Analysis: When building long-term portfolio, selecting stocks, value investing
  • Use Technical Analysis: For timing entries/exits, short-term trading, managing risk
  • Combine Both: Many successful investors use fundamentals for stock selection and technicals for timing

Getting Started with Each

Fundamental Analysis First Steps

  1. Learn to read balance sheets and P&L statements
  2. Study key ratios (P/E, Debt/Equity, ROE)
  3. Analyze management quality and moats
  4. Start with bluechip companies before analyzing midcaps

Technical Analysis First Steps

  1. Learn basic chart patterns (support/resistance, trends)
  2. Master 2-3 key indicators (e.g., RSI, moving averages)
  3. Practice on historical charts first
  4. Start with longer timeframes before day trading
